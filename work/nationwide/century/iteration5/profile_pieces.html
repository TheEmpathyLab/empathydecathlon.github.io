Concern #1: How will my heart health impact my retirement?


My family has a history of heart disease. I'm worried--is that going to derail my retirement savings?


Strategy #1: Uncovered my health risks by taking a health screening

Through a retirement health screening, I found I have unhealthy cholesterol levels and a very high chance of heart disease in retirement.


Strategy #2: Predicted my personalized health expenses


I used the info from my health screening to help me predict my future health expenses--and holy cow! My high risk of heart disease was going to cost me an extra $88,000 in medical expenses. Not cool.

Strategy #3: Worked with a health coach to understand my health predictors


I worked with a health coach to understand my predictors--the things I could do to keep me healthy and curb this potential expense. They analyzed my diet, exercise and healthcare know-how to show me ways I could invest in my health.

Did you know?

Working with a medical professional to screen for key health issues can help you take action to reduce your risks for certain diseases and catch issues early—helping you save lots of money in retirement.
