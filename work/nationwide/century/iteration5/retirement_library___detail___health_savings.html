Health Savings

The most common health savings accounts available are: Health Savings Account (HSA), Flexible Savings Account (FSA), Health Reimbursement Account (HRA), and Retirement Reimbursement Account (RRA).

What are the common health savings accounts that people are using to save for health expenses?


An HSA is a savings account that you self-fund (or is funded by your employer) that you can use to pay for health expenses. Other similar accounts include an FSA, HRA, and RRA (See terminology below).

What is a Health Savings Account (HSA)?

You may want a medical savings account because you don't pay taxes when money goes in or out. You can invest your HSA like you would your 401(k), and any gains you earn are also tax-free.

Why should I use an HSA?


Contribution limit - the maximum amount you can contribute to a medical savings account each year while still receiving tax-advantaged treatment. For 2018, the HSA contribution limit is $3450/individuals, $6850/family per year. If you're over 55, you can make an additional $1000 per year.

Terminology

Health Savings Accounts (HSAs) - HSAs are tax-advantaged medical savings accounts available to United States taxpayers who are enrolled in a High Deductible Health Plan.

High deductible health plan (HDHP) – A high deductible health plan is a health insurance policy that features higher deductibles and lower premiums than traditional insurance plans. HDHPs can be combined with a health savings account or a health reimbursement arrangement that allows for payment of qualified out-of-pocket medical expenses on a pre-tax basis. Covers expenses either before or during retirement.

Flexible Savings Account (FSA) – Similar to an HSA, but funds do not roll over year over year and are lost if not spent in the year they are contributed. Unlike an HSA, FSA’s are controlled by the employer. Covers expenses either before or during retirement.

Health Reimbursement Account (HRA) – Similar to an HSA, but is controlled by the employer. Covers expenses either before or during retirement. 

Retirement Reimbursement Account (RRA) – an employer-controlled account that you can use that covers only expenses incurred in retirement.
