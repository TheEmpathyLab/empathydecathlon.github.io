Delaying Retirement Seemed to be the Only Way to Bridge the Gap


Here is what Ursula found

Ursula had a tough decision to make. Every other health insurance option costs over $9,000 more—but COBRA only allows you to keep your employer's coverage for only 18 months after you leave. If Ursula was going use this cost effective option, she would have to wait an additional 1.5 years to retire—having to keep working till she was 63.5.

Actions

Learnings

Ursula continued to work for 1.5 more years to qualify for COBRA

Put away an additional $25,000 in her 401k during her extra 1.5 years of work

Once she was 63.5, Ursula worked with a Coverage Coach to help her sign up for COBRA online

Saved a total of $12,750 in healthcare expenses compared the next most affordable option

To pay for Medicare Premiums, Ursula rolled her IRA into a Tax-Free Health Annuity

Annual tax savings of $1,000 and $12,000 per year in protected lifetime income

Saving Big by Working a Bit Longer Than Planned

Because of picking COBRA, Ursula waited an additional 1.5 years to retire (we know, we know—it kind of stinks). But on the plus-side, this let her really focus on stowing away some bonus savings into her 401k! She saved an extra $25,000.

Spending 25% of Retirement Account on Medical Expenses, Tax-Free


When she turned 65, Ursula rolled-over the $200,000 she had built up in her IRA into a Health Annuity. This allows her to use 25% of the $1,000 monthly payments on healthcare expenses, tax-free. She is setting up the annuity to auto-pay her $250 in various Medicare, Medicgap and Medicare supplemental premiums each month, tax-free, for the rest of her life. She'll save over $1,000 each year in taxes, will never have to remember to pay Medicare premiums and will get a monthly check for $750 for the rest of her life.
